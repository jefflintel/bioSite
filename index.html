Adam Blowers

I have no idea how I got here

Adam developed a passion about cars from a young age working with his dad.

Adam's dad is a retired welder and spent his whole life working with his hands and fixing things. From a very young age Adam spent time working on cars with his dad and discovering how they worked. He was fascinated by how everything was interconnected. That love of vehicles continues today. His past fleet of vehicles has included everything from Supras to Subarus. He currently has a 1992 Mitsubishi Montero SR that he's in the process of converting for overlanding. He also has his eye on a Land Rover Discovery II and would like to own a 200-series Toyota Landcruiser someday.

Adam loves bikes - because they're awesome!

Adam finds bicycles interesting because they're something mechanical that most people can work on easily. He has been interested in vintage bicycles for several years. Right now he has a Bridgestone X0-3, a Trek 520, and a Scott Scale Pro. His modern collection includes a Trek 920 and a Kona Unit. He can tell you all about vintage Trek color schemes and components. Adam is the executive director at the Community Bike Project Omaha and has been working on extending the programming reach into more of Omaha and the surrounding area.

Growing up in Tennessee Adam quickly learned he loved the outdoors.

Growing up in Morristown, Tennessee shaped Adam's love of the outdoors. The eastern part of Tennessee is different enough from the western and central parts that people commonly mention which area they're from. Eastern Tennessee has a lot of mountains and woods. In fact, Great Smoky Mountains National Park is about an hour away from Adam's hometown. As he got older and traveled more Adam got interested in hiking and backpacking to experience the beauty of nature. He's also gone on several bikepacking trips locally, combining his love of the outdoors and bikes. Adam took a several week bikepacking trip across Montana, something he really enjoyed.
